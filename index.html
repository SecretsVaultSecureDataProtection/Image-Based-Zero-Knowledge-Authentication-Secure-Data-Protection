Concept

A spellbook of light for your secrets

Secrets Vault binds cryptographic strength to something humans truly remember: images. Any picture – a skyline, a sketch, a frame from a movie – can be transformed into a high-entropy key and used as an authentication factor or encryption source. How it works

From image to incorruptible key

Inside the Vault, photons become numbers, numbers become keys, and keys become gates that only your users can open. Below is a simplified, human-readable view of the protocol – the true spellbook is encoded in math.

01 · Edge capture

The user selects or captures an image on their device. The Vault SDK processes it locally, extracting robust visual features that survive typical compression, scaling, and minor edits.

02 · Feature constellation

Those features are mapped into a high-entropy vector and combined with device-specific and context-specific factors. No raw image is stored or sent; only derived values pass deeper into the protocol.

03 · Post-quantum key weaving

A post-quantum key derivation function transforms that vector into one or more cryptographic keys, suitable for authentication, encryption, or both. Entropy is amplified; correlations are sealed off from adversaries.

04 · Zero-knowledge exchange

During login, the client proves knowledge of the correct image-derived key through a zero-knowledge style interaction. Servers validate without ever learning enough to impersonate the user.

05 · Encrypted realms

The same image-bound keys can encrypt sensitive payloads – secrets, files, wallets, or recovery data – into Vaults. Decryption is only possible for holders of the image (and optional second factors) under your policy rules.

In code, it feels like a simple SDK call: vault.deriveKeyFromImage(image).authenticate() – yet behind it lies a multi-layered cryptographic universe.
